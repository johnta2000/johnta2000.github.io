Finals Preparation

The layout of the Spring 2019 Final was a two-hour exam comprised of 10 questions. The average (from the better performing section) was a 74%, but the standard deviation for the exam is unknown. From memory, the types of questions that were asked are listed below:

    • Heat equation
    • ??? I can't remember :(
    • Fourier series of function
    • Wave equation
    • Fourier transform on infinite domain
    • Sturm-Liouville problem, with use of Rayleigh quotient
    • Sturm-Liouville problem, proving that the given eigenfunction exists, and that eigenvalue is NOT smallest
    • Annulus
    • Eigenfunction expansion
    • Cylinder

Best way to prepare for this exam was to run through the most recent 241 finals, and to do the final exam review questions. Understanding S-L theory and how to use the Rayleigh quotient is essentially free points. Techniques of separation of variables and to find coefficients are also free points. Solutions are posted for the Spring and Fall 2016 finals, and are extremely useful--I had to crowdsource my answers during the semester in which I took 241.
