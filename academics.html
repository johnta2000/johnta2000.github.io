High School


Project Thesis
Plastics are one of mankind's most important inventions; their value is seen in everyday life and in the economy. However, many plastics have a variety of problems. About 33 million tons of plastic are produced each year, but only about 7% of it is recycled. The problem with plastic waste is compounded by the fact that plastics do not biodegrade quickly. The goal was to control and facilitate biodegradability in existing polymers. Silicone rubber and polylactic acid (PLA) were surface derivatized using alkoxysilanes. A goniometer and tensile testing apparatuses were used to perform contact angle goniometry and tensile tests, respectively, to examine the effects of the alkoxysilanes. Additional PLA samples were surface modified in order to subject them to bacterial testing using Staphylococcus epidermidis at 30 Â°C for three and six day periods. During immersion, the number of bacteria was determined using a spectrometer. After removal from the medium, the specimens were tensile tested in order to determine the effects of the S. epidermidis on the polymer. There was a significant difference between the ultimate tensile strength of the APDMS surface bonded to PLA that was not in bacteria compared to the samples that were immersed in bacteria for 3 days (p = 0.0343) and 6 days (p = 0.0049). The results suggest that surface bonding initially strengthened the material, but after exposure to bacteria, the material became weaker more quickly. This procedure could be applied on a mass scale in plastic production plants to shorten biodegradation time of waste that is deposited into the environment, which would minimize the accumulation of future plastic waste.
